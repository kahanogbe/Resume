Intro

| Who am I ?

I am a PhD candidate in electrical engineering. I am working on three-terminal perovskite on silicon tandem modeling, simulation, characterization and optimization. Three-terminal tandem cell is a novel concept of multijunction solar cells developed to achieve high efficiency over the theoretical limit of a single solar cell.

| Where do i work ?

i have prepared my PhD in The GeePs lab, in the team "Materials", where imminent researchers have developed and patent a new concept called three-terminal-Selective Band Offset Barrier (3T-SBOB). The novelty of this 3T-SBOB design lies in the independent operation of two monolithically fabricated cells. This means that the two subcells have independent operating voltage and can operate at maximum power points determined by their respective bandgaps. The design therefore delivers tandem efficiencies equal to conventionnal four-terminal designs, but without the constraints of two-terminal and four-terminal designs. The expected three-terminal tandem efficiency, with optimized subcell bandgaps, is over 30%.

| My famous contribution ?

I brought an innovative solution to the problem of the interconnection of 3T tandem cells within solar modules, for which no industrialized model exists yet. The original architecture is a flexible, functional, and robust architecture for interconnecting complementary 3T tandem cells of reversed polarity in a two-terminal "unit block. This novel "interconnection" method was patented in 2022. It is a original way to fabricate Solar module.

Work & Publications

| Modeling & Simulation

During my PhD, I simulated: silicon simple junction solar cells, Silicon interdigitated back contact solar cells (Si-IBC), Perovskite solar cells (PSC), III-V solar cells, CIGS solar cells, and tandem solar cells using TCAD-SILVACO software. Particular attention is given to the three-terminal tandem solar cells.

Optical & electrical Characterization

I performed fundamental electrical characterization of solar cells: Current-voltage measurements. I did advanced optical and optoelectronic characterization ( Absorption, spectral response, transmission, and Quantum efficiency ) using adapted Fourier Transformed-infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR) plateform.
